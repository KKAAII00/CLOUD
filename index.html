About Penang

Early Settlement

Penang was founded in 1786 by Captain Francis Light and became an important trading port in Southeast Asia.

Colonial Era

Penang flourished under British administration and developed into a multicultural trading hub.

Modern Penang

Today Penang is famous for its heritage sites, food culture, tourism and technology industries.

Historical Timeline

  • 1786: Captain Francis Light established Penang as a British trading post.
  • 1805: Penang became the fourth presidency of British India.
  • 1826: Penang joined Malacca and Singapore to form the Straits Settlements.
  • 1941: Japanese forces occupied Penang during World War II.
  • 1957: Malaya gained independence from British rule.
  • 2008: George Town was recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
